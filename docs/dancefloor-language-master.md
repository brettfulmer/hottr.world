Amharic uses the Ge'ez script, one of the oldest alphabets still in use — dating back to the 5th century BC. Unlike Arabic and Hebrew, it reads left-to-right. Amharic has unique "ejective" consonants produced with a glottalic airstream that don't exist in any European language.

**VERSION NOTES**
